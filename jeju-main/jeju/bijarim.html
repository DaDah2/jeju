Bijarim

Forest

Introduction

Designated Natural Monument No. 374, Bijarim Forest is a group of
about 2,800 nutmeg yew trees between 500 and 800 years old.
The trees measure 7-14 meters in height and 50-110 centimeters in
diameter, with canopy spread of 10-15 meters. In the past, the trees
were harvested for pine nuts, as well as used for the construction of
quality furniture and baduk game boards.
